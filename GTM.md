# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target User Persona

**Primary: The Pragmatic Engineering Manager**
- Title: Engineering Manager, VP Engineering, CTO at a 15–100 person company
- Pain: Gets a $4k/month AI tools invoice, has no idea if it's justified, doesn't have time to research every tool's pricing
- Behavior: Googles "is Cursor Business worth it" or "ChatGPT Team vs Plus for small team" — lands on Reddit threads with conflicting answers
- Budget authority: Yes, or direct line to someone who has it
- Discovery: Twitter/X, Hacker News, LinkedIn, word of mouth from other EMs

**Secondary: The Cost-Conscious Founder**
- Title: Founder/CTO at a 5–20 person startup
- Pain: Paying for AI tools out of pocket or from a tight runway, wants to make sure every dollar is justified
- Behavior: Audits every SaaS subscription quarterly
- Discovery: Indie Hackers, Twitter, startup Slack communities

## First 100 Users Strategy

**Week 1–2: Manual outreach (target: 30 users)**

Post in communities where the target user already is:

1. **Hacker News Show HN** — "Show HN: I built a free tool to audit your AI tooling spend"
   - Post on a Tuesday or Wednesday morning (9am ET)
   - Keep the post honest: what it does, what it doesn't do, what's next
   - Respond to every comment within the first 2 hours

2. **r/ExperiencedDevs and r/cscareerquestions** — post a genuine question first ("how do you manage AI tool costs for your team?"), then mention the tool in a follow-up comment after getting engagement

3. **Twitter/X** — post a thread: "I audited our team's AI spend and found $400/month in waste. Here's what I found and the tool I built to check yours:"
   - Include a screenshot of the results page
   - Tag 2–3 engineering managers you know personally

4. **LinkedIn** — same thread format, slightly more professional tone
   - Engineering managers are more active on LinkedIn than Twitter

**Week 3–4: Community seeding (target: 70 more users)**

- Post in relevant Slack communities: Rands Leadership Slack, Software Lead Weekly community, various startup Slack groups
- Reach out directly to 20 engineering managers on LinkedIn with a personalized note — not a pitch, just "built this, thought you might find it useful"
- Submit to newsletters: TLDR, Software Lead Weekly, Pointer.io

## Acquisition Channels (Ranked by Expected ROI)

1. **SEO — long-tail keywords** (highest ROI, slowest)
   - "cursor pro vs business", "chatgpt team vs plus", "github copilot enterprise worth it"
   - These are high-intent searches with weak existing content
   - Write one comparison post per tool pair, link to the audit tool

2. **Twitter/X organic** (medium ROI, fast)
   - Engineering managers share cost-saving tools
   - One good tweet from a 10k+ follower EM can drive 200+ signups

3. **Hacker News** (high variance, one-time)
   - A good Show HN can drive 500–2000 visitors in 24 hours
   - Doesn't compound but builds initial credibility

4. **Product Hunt** (medium, one-time)
   - Good for a launch spike and backlink
   - Less relevant for B2B tools than it used to be

5. **Paid (not yet)**
   - Don't run ads until the conversion funnel is validated
   - CAC via paid will be $50–200 for this audience — only makes sense with a clear monetization path

## Founder Outreach Strategy

Direct outreach to 50 engineering managers in week 1:

- Find targets via LinkedIn (search: "Engineering Manager" + company size 20–100)
- Message template:
  > "Hey [name] — built a free tool that audits AI tooling spend and flags where teams are overpaying. No signup required, takes 2 minutes. Thought it might be useful given [specific thing about their company]. [link]"
- Keep it under 3 sentences. No pitch. No ask beyond "try it."
- Follow up once after 5 days if no response. Then stop.

Goal: 10% response rate → 5 users from direct outreach. The real value is the feedback, not the signups.

## Realistic Traction Metrics

**Week 1 targets:**
- 50 audit completions
- 10 lead captures (email submissions)
- 1 piece of genuine user feedback

**Month 1 targets:**
- 300 audit completions
- 60 lead captures (20% conversion from audit to lead)
- 3 users who share the audit URL with a colleague

**Month 3 targets:**
- 1,000 audit completions/month
- 200 leads in Supabase
- 1 inbound request for a "team version" or API access (signal for next product)

**Pivot trigger:** If after 500 audits, fewer than 5% of users submit the lead capture form, the value proposition isn't landing. Either the savings numbers are too small to motivate action, or the lead form is asking for too much too soon.
